My Education

An overview of my education

Stevens Institute of Technology

If you look in the Meriam-Webster dictionary for the phrase "nose-to-the-grindstone" you'll find a picture of Castle Point at Stevens. My favorite class that really pushed me to my mathematical limits was Multivariate Optimization with Darinka Dentcheva. Just like programming has its own mindset, so does optimization and spatial thinking and, man-oh-man, did that class really ingrain a new way of thinking in my head.

My favorite memory thus far was Freshman year during a massive blizzard that covered campus in 24 inches of snow. The memories made in those ensuing snow days were some of the richest yet.

Universiteit van Amsterdam

Last semester I studied abroad in Amsterdam, Netherlands. Although I took relatively few classes while I was there I did enjoy the Behavioural Economics class that spent much time discussing how people behave under different conditions.

Not quite sure there was one memory here that really stood out. I mean, when else in your life will you ever get a chance to have few (if any) responsibilities and be able travel around, engage in shenanigans and live in one of the most exuberant cities on Earth? The entire semester is one incredible memory.

Oratory Preparatory School

Oratory was where I attended highschool, located in Summit, NJ. Among my favorite classes here was Physics. But, ironically enough, it is not my favorite because I was good at it nor because I enjoyed it. Instead, I loathed the subject so much so that it made me realize I could not pursue a major in Mechanical Engineering and instead drove me towards Quantitative Finance.

My favorite memories at Oratory were those I made playing on the lacrosse team.
